First Post

May 30, 2022

What?

Why, hello there. My name is Edward (which you probably already knew one way or another).

You might be wondering why the first section of the first post starts with “Why?” but let’s talk about that later.

So, the tl;dr is that I’ve decided to start a machine learning blog. Hopefully, this side-gig lasts longer than my last one…

I’m mostly going to be writing some articles about random topics I either encountered online or topics I didn’t know about and happened to stumble across when I had a random idea for a machine learning algorithm (I bet you most of them are useless, but it’s fun implementing them anyways and seeing how they perform).

I might also write about other things I’m learning about, and those topics will likely extend broadly to math, computer science, and maybe even stuff such as blockchain and cryptocurrency (still learning the baby steps on this one).

Most of the posts will probably follow the same structure as this post, i.e.:

  • What?
    • The section where I talk about what I’m going to talk about.
  • Why?
    • The section where how the topic I’m going to talk about is interesting or worth knowing about (in my own words, so might not be perfect).
  • How?
    • The section where I talk about how the thing we’ve been talking about up until now actually works. The previous three sections should be significantly shorter than this one and they should mostly not be math-heavy (as it’ll take waaay too much time to write everything down).
  • Additional Notes
    • Any additional stuff I want to mention.
  • Resource List
    • A list of resources I looked through to make the post.
  • Final Notes
    • Just me saying bye. With all that said, I’ll try to follow this structure in my first post and consider it as practice for writing a blog.

Why?

In recent times, I’ve realized that I’ve been bulldozering through a lot of interesting things where I read through resources in a frenzy but forget everything I read in a few months. I hope that this blog can help me concretize the things I learn and also serve as a reference for future me when I forget the specifics of what I learned.

How?

Hmm, I might’ve lied when I said this section should be significantly longer than the other posts.

The “How” for this post is pretty self-explanatory; I’ll just be be posting stuff here once in a while (I’m not sure about the frequency yet).

I’m not sure how I’ll be organizing the “How?” for future sections as I’m sure the optimal structure will vary depending on the specific topic, so I’ll keep it flexible but hopefully they’ll still be logically easy to follow.
